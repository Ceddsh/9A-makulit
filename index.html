About Endangered Species in the Philippines

The Philippines is home to an incredibly diverse range of flora and fauna. However, due to deforestation, habitat loss, and illegal hunting, many species in the country are now at risk of extinction. Efforts are being made by conservationists and environmental groups to protect and save these endangered species from disappearing forever.

Endangered Species in the Philippines

Tamaraw

Native to Mindoro Island, the Tamaraw is one of the most critically endangered animals in the Philippines.

Philippine Eagle

Known as the national bird of the Philippines, the Philippine eagle is critically endangered due to deforestation and hunting.

Whale Shark

The whale shark is the largest living fish species, known for its gentle nature and distinctive pattern of spots and stripes. Found in tropical waters around the world, the whale shark is a migratory species and is particularly present in the Philippines, where they can be seen in places like Oslob, Cebu.
